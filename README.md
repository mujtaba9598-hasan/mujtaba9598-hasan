# 💫 About Me:
# ⚡️ Syed Mujtaba Hasan<br>**Lead Web Architect & Prompt Engineer | UAE & Pakistan**<br><br>Welcome to my GitHub! I specialize in architecting "State of the Art" websites and engineering highly specific AI prompts to build next-gen digital experiences. I reject standard tropes and bloated plugins, focusing instead on high-interactivity, out-of-the-box concepts like Kinetic Branding, Scrollytelling, and physics-based micro-interactions.<br><br>### 🔭 What I'm Currently Working On<br>* **High-Traffic Utility Fleet:** Deploying a massive fleet of lightweight, highly optimized utility websites utilizing strictly HTML5 and custom PHP.<br>* **Fintech Mobile App:** Architecting an Android application focused on accounting, journal entries, and cash flow forecasting.<br>* **B2B Digital Transformations:** Crafting next-gen, glassmorphism-inspired UI/UX for technical services and industrial businesses across Dubai and Sharjah.<br>* **Agent-Based Workflows:** Orchestrating specialized AI agents to streamline complex coding, design, and building processes.<br><br>### 🛠 Tech Stack & Toolkit<br>* **Frontend Core:** HTML5, Tailwind CSS, Modern JavaScript<br>* **Motion & 3D:** GSAP (ScrollTrigger, Flip), Lenis (Smooth Scroll), Three.js, React Spring, Framer Motion<br>* **Backend & Logic:** PHP, SQL<br>* **Design & Prompts:** Nano Banana Pro (Art Direction & Textures), Anti Gravity (Code Generation)<br>* **Hardware Environment:** Mac mini 2024<br><br>### 🧠 Design Philosophy & Protocols<br>* **The Peak-End Rule:** Every micro-interaction must have a climax and a physical, damping resolution.<br>* **Kinetic Identity:** Designing UIs where the brand's identity dictates the grid, using SVG filters and seamless state changes.<br>* **Scrollytelling:** Treating products as protagonists through state-based animations, sticky positioning, and exploded wireframe views.<br>* **Anti-Standard:** Dark themes, liquid metal textures, and aluminum finishes over standard corporate blue/white templates.<br><br>### 📫 Let's Connect<br>*Whether it's discussing custom WebGL shaders, optimal prompt architecture, or scaling high-performance web systems, I'm always open to connecting.*<br>
